On this page

  • One-minute questions

After each class session, you are expected to give a brief summary of what happened in that session as well as pointing to things that either 1) were particularly meaningful to you or 2) that you found confusing.

These are called one-minute questions to indicate roughly how much time you should spend on your answer. Of course, you are welcome to spend more time. But even if you spend just one minute, your answer should be honest, articulate, and aimed at improving the class for yourself and your student colleagues.

DO NOT write your answer while still in class.
